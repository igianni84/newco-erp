## Decision: A status-bearing scope is `suspended` iff covered by ≥1 active Hold — recomputed from Hold coverage, not tracked by provenance

The Hold→`suspended` coupling (the headline seam `parties-holds` and `parties-membership-activation` left open) is realised as a **coverage-recompute** model:

- A status-bearing scope — **Customer**, **Account**, **Profile** — is in `suspended` (Profile: `Suspended`) **iff** at least one **active** Hold covers it. "Covers" = an active Hold on that exact scope, **plus** — for a Profile — an active **Customer-scope** Hold on its owning Customer (BR-K-Hold-3 cascade); Profile-scope Holds isolate (BR-K-Hold-4); Account-scope Holds isolate to the Account.
- **Placing** a Hold drives every **covered scope currently in its suspendable from-state** (`active` / Profile `Active`) to `suspended`, recording the suspension event in the same transaction. **Lifting** a Hold drives every covered scope **currently `suspended`** back to `active` — recording the reactivation event — **iff no other active Hold still covers it** (coverage is re-queried on lift; "the triggering Hold" of § 10.1 is read as "the last covering Hold").
- The suspend/restore transitions are **explicit within-module Actions** — `SuspendCustomer` / `ReactivateCustomer`, `SuspendProfile` / `ReactivateProfile`, `SuspendAccount` / `ReactivateAccount` — each the sole writer of its status field, from-state guarded, emitting the verbatim § 15 event. The Hold place/lift paths (`PlaceHold`, `LiftHold`, and the system `kyc`-lift in `RecordKycVerified`) **invoke** these Actions; the Actions are also directly invocable (operator-initiated "manual" suspension, AC-K-BR-Customer-1 *"manual or via Hold"*).
- A **Customer-scope** Hold's suspend/restore **cascades** to the Customer's Profiles: `SuspendCustomer` suspends the Customer and each of its `Active` Profiles (one `ProfileSuspended` each); `ReactivateCustomer` restores the Customer and each Profile **that is itself no longer covered** by any active Hold (its own Profile-scope Hold, or another Customer-scope Hold, holds it suspended).
- **Closure** (`CloseCustomer`, `CloseAccount`) is **operator-only**, never Hold-driven (a Hold is reversible; closure is terminal). `ActivateAccount` does **not** exist — the Account is born `active`, so its only `→ active` edge is `ReactivateAccount` (restore), never a birth activation.

## Context: Why this came up

The shipped Hold registry (`parties-holds`) records Holds but performs **no** status transition — `PlaceHold`/`LiftHold` carry an explicit *"deferred demand-side seam"* docblock, and the *Birth States Recorded, Lifecycle Transitions Deferred* requirement defers "the Hold→`suspended` **status** coupling … (placing a Hold records the Hold but performs no status transition)". `parties-membership-activation` shipped the `→ Active` edges (Customer `pending → active`, Profile `Approved → Active`) but explicitly deferred `Active → Suspended` and the Hold coupling to **this** change (`parties-membership-suspension`). The PRD requires the coupling to be **bidirectional and cascading**: § 4.2.1 drives Profile `Active → Suspended` from "a Profile-level Hold … or a Customer-level Hold … that cascades to all of the Customer's Profiles"; § 10.1 restores on lift; AC-K-FSM-9 says "Account-level Holds drive `active → suspended`; lift drives `suspended → active`"; § 15.1 says `CustomerSuspended` "Cascades to all the Customer's Profiles". BR-K-Hold-1 admits **multiple concurrent** Holds per scope. Something must decide *when a lift actually un-suspends* given multiple Holds — that decision is this ADR.

## Alternatives considered

- **(A) Provenance tracking** — record which Hold suspended which scope (a `suspended_by_hold_id` or a join row), reverse exactly that on the Hold's lift. *Rejected:* needs new state, and under BR-K-Hold-1 multi-Hold / Customer→Profile cascade it answers "should this scope return to active now?" worse than simply re-querying coverage — two Holds suspending one scope, or a Customer Hold + a Profile Hold on the same Profile, force bookkeeping that coverage-recompute gets for free. It also duplicates truth already in `parties_holds`.
- **(B) `PlaceHold` writes status inline (no explicit Suspend Action)** — mirror `RecordKycVerified`'s inline cross-entity write. *Rejected:* the shipped guard tests **pre-name** `SuspendCustomer` / `SuspendAccount` / `CloseCustomer` / `CloseAccount` as real future Actions (the forbidden-Action list un-forbids exactly these), AC-K-BR-Customer-1 wants a **manual** (operator, no-Hold) suspension path, and a single FSM writer per transition keeps the from-state guard + event emission testable in one place. The coupling *invokes* the Action; it does not duplicate it. (`RecordKycVerified` writes inline only because the per-type lift discipline (ADR 2026-06-18) forbids it from reusing `LiftHold` — a different constraint.)
- **(C) Logical-only suspension** — a Customer Hold leaves Profile `state` `Active` and the read-API (`DatabaseComplianceStatusReader`) reports them blocked via the BR-K-Hold-3 cascade; no `Profile.state` write. *Rejected:* § 4.2.1 (L190) and § 15.1 (L752, `CustomerSuspended` "Cascades to all the Customer's Profiles") explicitly require the Profile to **physically transition** to `Suspended` and emit `ProfileSuspended` on the cascade. The read-API cascade and the status cascade are both required and are not substitutes.

## Reasoning: Why coverage-recompute won

Suspended-ness is, semantically, "is this scope under any active restriction?" — exactly what an active-Hold-coverage query answers. Making it a **derived** property (recomputed on every place/lift) means the status can never disagree with the Hold table, needs no extra schema, and handles multiple concurrent Holds and the Customer→Profile cascade uniformly. The coverage query already exists and is the canonical cascade logic: `DatabaseComplianceStatusReader::activeHoldTypesForScopes()` resolves "(Profile-scope) OR (its Customer-scope)" coverage with Profile isolation — the suspend/restore coupling re-uses that scope-coverage shape (re-querying Hold rows under lock, since it writes). Explicit Actions as the sole writers preserve the established Module-K idiom (from-state guard → `lockForUpdate` → `update` → `record` in one transaction; the `RecordKycVerified` cross-entity-in-one-transaction precedent) and give the operator-manual path a home. From-state guarding the coupling means a Hold on a **non-suspendable** scope (the `kyc` Hold auto-placed on a `pending` Customer at onboarding) records the Hold and drives **no** transition — onboarding KYC never "suspends", preserving the compliance-FSM-independence the shipped `ComplianceIndependenceTest` enforces (the FSM verdict drives the **Hold**; the Hold — not the verdict — drives status, and only from `active`).

## Trade-offs accepted

- **Restore re-queries coverage on every lift** (one indexed `parties_holds` read per affected scope; the Customer cascade re-evaluates each Profile). Cheap, and correct under multi-Hold; the alternative's bookkeeping is the worse trade.
- **`RecordKycVerified` gains a restore step** — after lifting the active `kyc` Hold(s) it must recompute coverage and `ReactivateCustomer` if the Customer is now uncovered (a no-op at onboarding, where the Customer was `pending`, never suspended). A shipped Action grows; acceptable (it is app code, the coupling is its responsibility).
- **`CloseCustomer` does not cascade-cancel Profiles.** § 15.1 `CustomerClosed` names **no** Profile cascade (unlike `CustomerSuspended`, which names one); per zero-invention, closure transitions only the Customer. Profile resolution at closure is left to the spec's silence — not invented here.
- **Account-scope coverage is Account-only.** The read-API explicitly does not resolve an Account→Profile or Account→Customer cascade (the PRD specifies none); the suspend/restore coupling matches — an Account Hold suspends only the Account.
