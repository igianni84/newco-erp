## Why

The Customer console already renders the demand-side compliance context — **status**, **KYC status**, **sanctions status**, Account status and Profiles — read-only, and the Holds-first slice (`archive/2026-06-22-operator-console-parties-holds`) shipped the first compliance **write** surface (place / lift). KYC and sanctions are the two remaining compliance axes the console shows but cannot yet act on. Module K's KYC and sanctions domain Actions are shipped and unchanged; Admin Panel §3.K names "Manage a KYC verification" and "Review a sanctions-screening match" as the operator capabilities. This change adds those two write surfaces, completing the Customer compliance console.

## What Changes

- **ADD three form-less KYC header actions** on `ViewCustomer` — **Require KYC** (`RequireKyc`, `not_required | unset → pending`), **Record KYC verified** (`RecordKycVerified`, `pending → verified`) and **Record KYC rejected** (`RecordKycRejected`, `pending → rejected`) — each invoking its domain Action and each **visible iff** `kyc_status` sits in that verb's legal from-state (visibility = the complement of the domain from-state guard, so a rejected transition is unreachable through the surface). `RequireKyc` auto-places a Customer-scope `kyc` Hold (which, via the shipped Hold coupling, suspends an `active` Customer); `RecordKycVerified` auto-lifts it (and reactivates the Customer when no other Hold still covers it); `RecordKycRejected` is audit-only and the `kyc` Hold remains. **None of the three records a KYC-specific domain event** — the only events are the coupled `CustomerHoldPlaced` / `CustomerHoldLifted` and the status events `CustomerSuspended` / `CustomerReactivated`. There is **no** Customer KYC "waive" verb (no such domain Action — only producer KYC is waivable).
- **ADD one form-bearing sanctions header action** — **Record sanctions screening** (`RecordCustomerScreening`) — collecting a `verdict` (`SanctionsStatus`: `pending | passed | failed | under_review`) and a `trigger_source` (restricted to the operator-selectable `onboarding` and `compliance_ad_hoc`; the automated `cadence` / `aml_threshold` sources are **not** offered). Recording sets `sanctions_status`, `last_screening_at`, `next_rescreen_at` (+12 months) and `screening_trigger_source`, and records exactly **one** of `CustomerOnboardingScreeningPassed` / `CustomerOnboardingScreeningFailed` / `CustomerRescreeningPassed` / `CustomerRescreeningFailed` by verdict × source — a `pending` / `under_review` verdict records **no** event (state still updates). `onboarding` is **first-screening-only**: once `last_screening_at` is set it is dropped from the form options, and a domain re-onboarding attempt is rejected (`IllegalSanctionsTransition::onboardingAlreadyScreened`).
- **MODIFY the two shipped Customer requirements** to drop **KYC** and **sanctions** from their "no write affordance" / "no KYC/sanctions action" negatives — those surfaces now exist (cross-referenced to the two new requirements). The read-only **rendering** of the KYC and sanctions badges is unchanged; **Account-lifecycle and Profile-lifecycle** negatives remain (still deferred).
- **EN + IT localization** for the new KYC + sanctions keys + extension of the Customer console i18n completeness test; a **PG17 closing-chain integration test** that drives the cross-slice KYC → Hold coupling **through the surface the Holds slice shipped** — `RequireKyc` makes a `kyc` Hold row appear in the Holds table (auto-managed → no per-row Lift) and suspends the Customer; `RecordKycVerified` flips that row to `lifted` and reactivates the Customer.
- **No** new domain code, migration, or composer dependency. **No** `ModuleBoundariesTest` change — the operand-enum carve-out already admits the `Parties\Enums` prefix for OperatorPanel; this slice merely exercises it for `SanctionsStatus` / `ScreeningTriggerSource`. `KycStatus` is a **state** enum (rendered / predicated via its cast `->value`, never imported).

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities

_None._ This slice extends the existing `operator-console` capability; the KYC and sanctions domain behaviour already lives in `party-registry` (consumed read-only, not modified).

### Modified Capabilities

- `operator-console`: **ADD** two requirements — _Operator manages a Customer's KYC verification through the console_ and _Operator records a Customer's sanctions screening through the console_. **MODIFY** two existing requirements — _Operator advances a Customer through its status lifecycle_ and _The Customer console surfaces the orthogonal compliance and membership context read-only_ — to remove the now-false "no KYC/sanctions write affordance" assertions (Account / Profile remain deferred).

## Impact

- **Console code:** `app/Modules/OperatorPanel/Filament/Resources/Parties/CustomerResource/Pages/ViewCustomer.php` — three form-less KYC header actions (via the kit's `lifecycleAction()`) + one bespoke sanctions screening form action (via `surfaceLifecycleOutcome()`, the `placeHold` precedent). `CustomerResource.php` is unchanged (the KYC / sanctions badges already render).
- **i18n:** `lang/en/operator_console.php` + `lang/it/operator_console.php` (extend the `customer` block); the IT value of every new key differs from EN (the IT-differs guard).
- **Tests:** new console behaviour tests + a PG17 closing-chain test under `tests/Feature/Modules/OperatorPanel/Parties/`; the stale `assertActionDoesNotExist('requireKyc')` / sanctions absence guards in `CustomerLifecycleConsoleTest` updated (the **account** / **profile** absence guards stay — those remain deferred).
- **Domain (read-only, unchanged):** `Parties\Actions\{RequireKyc,RecordKycVerified,RecordKycRejected,RecordCustomerScreening}`, `Models\Customer`, `Enums\{KycStatus,SanctionsStatus,ScreeningTriggerSource}`, `Events\{CustomerOnboardingScreeningPassed,CustomerOnboardingScreeningFailed,CustomerRescreeningPassed,CustomerRescreeningFailed,CustomerHoldPlaced,CustomerHoldLifted,CustomerSuspended,CustomerReactivated}`, `Exceptions\{IllegalKycTransition,IllegalSanctionsTransition}`.
- **Slice boundary — deliberately NOT in this change** (each named to its slice):
  - **Enhanced-KYC** (the €10k / €50k threshold flag + timestamp) → deferred. §9.1 sets that flag by **auto-detection** (background job + order-completion trigger) with no separate state machine and **no operator domain Action** in the codebase; a manual toggle would need new Module K domain code (its own slice) and a console slice cannot write it without breaching the no-Eloquent-write-in-OperatorPanel invariant.
  - **Automated sanctions trigger sources** (`cadence` — the 12-month re-screen daily job; `aml_threshold` — the AML auto-rescreen) → deferred per §9.5 manual-first launch posture; not operator-selectable in the form.
  - **Direct Account verbs** (`SuspendAccount` / `ReactivateAccount` / `CloseAccount`) and **Profile lifecycle** → account / profile slices. (Account / Profile status moved by the KYC-driven **Hold coupling** is in scope here as a domain side-effect; the direct verbs are not.)
  - **The order-completion purchase gate** that reads `sanctions_status = passed` → Module S (§9.3 — Module K records the screening state, Module S enforces at checkout); out of this console's scope. **Anonymisation / GDPR erasure** → later slices.
