# Proposal — parties-holds

## Why

This is the **Hold-registry slice of Module K (Parties)** — the unified, trigger-agnostic account-restriction primitive that the compliance floor rests on (`Build_Workplan_v0.3-MVP.md` § Phase 2; Module_K_PRD § 4.8 "FLOOR"). It is on the critical path in two directions and closes one open loop:

- It **closes Phase 2 signoff** — *"Admin Panel functional for catalog + party + Hold workflows"* (`Build_Workplan_v0.3-MVP.md` § Phase 2). The Hold registry + place/lift + read-API are the Phase-2 (Module K) deliverable; the Filament console over it is a separate slice (below).
- It **opens the Phase 3+ enforcement gates** — *"Hold/sanctions enforcement at every transaction-initiation surface"* (`Build_Workplan_v0.3-MVP.md` § Phase 3; DEC-181). Module K exposes the read-API tuple; the downstream surfaces (Module S order-completion, C, E) consume it in their own phases — **not** in this slice.
- It **closes the KYC↔Hold loop** that `parties-compliance` left as a named seam: the shipped `party-registry` spec states *"The `kyc` Hold coupling (auto-place on KYC `pending`, auto-lift on `verified`) and the unified Hold registry SHALL remain deferred to `parties-holds`."* This change implements both.

The Hold registry is the prerequisite for every later transaction-initiation gate, and the only automatic Hold trigger that exists today (the `kyc` coupling onto the already-shipped KYC Actions) is wired here. Every other automatic trigger (`payment`/`fraud`/`compliance`/`credit`) depends on Module E/S signals that do not exist yet — those arrive with those modules; the registry is **trigger-agnostic** (AC-K-MVP-2), so they slot in without re-modelling.

It steps through **no open ADR gate**: domain events deliver **inline** at launch (`decisions/2026-06-12-event-substrate-and-audit-store.md`), so `CustomerHoldPlaced`/`CustomerHoldLifted` record like the spine `*Created` and screening events — no `queued` consumer. No object storage, no payment provider, no new dependency is touched. The one architectural decision the slice rests on — the **per-type Hold-lift discipline** — is recorded in ADR `decisions/2026-06-18-hold-lift-discipline-per-type.md` (which also flags that root `CLAUDE.md` Invariant #7 *"Holds are never auto-lifted"* is over-broad vs the spec: `kyc`/`payment` auto-lift, the other four do not).

## What Changes

- **Unified Hold entity** (PRD § 4.8; AC-K-FSM-10) — one new `parties_holds` table holding the registry. A Hold carries a `hold_type` (one of six — § 4.8), a `scope` (`scope_type` ∈ `customer | account | profile` + `scope_id`), a `status` (`active | lifted`), an optional placement `reason`, the placement actor + moment, and (on lift) the lift actor + moment + `lift_reason`. Module K is the **registry-of-record** for all Holds (DEC-168 — even finance-triggered ones, when those land).
- **Six Hold types** (PRD § 4.8; verbatim) — a `HoldType` enum `admin | kyc | payment | fraud | compliance | credit`, with an `autoLiftable(): bool` predicate (true for `kyc`/`payment` only — the enum's first predicate, mirroring `KycStatus::clears()`; ADR `2026-06-18-hold-lift-discipline-per-type.md`).
- **Three scopes + scope cascade** (PRD § 4.8 / § 4.8.1; AC-K-BR-Hold-3/4) — a `HoldScope` enum `customer | account | profile`. A **Customer-scope Hold cascades to all the Customer's Profiles** (BR-K-Hold-3); a **Profile-scope Hold isolates** (BR-K-Hold-4). Cascade is resolved at **read time** by the read-API (a Customer Hold is returned when interrogating any of that Customer's Profiles), not by writing duplicate Hold rows.
- **Place / lift Actions** (PRD § 4.8 / § 4.8.1; AC-K-FSM-10/11) — `PlaceHold` (operator, the manual-placement path — records `CustomerHoldPlaced`) and `LiftHold` (operator — records `CustomerHoldLifted`). `LiftHold` enforces the **per-type lift discipline**: it rejects lifting an `active` Hold whose type is **auto-managed** (`kyc`/`payment`) with a localized `IllegalHoldLift`, and rejects lifting an already-`lifted` Hold; `admin`/`fraud`/`compliance`/`credit` lift freely (AC-K-FSM-11).
- **`kyc` Hold coupling — the one automatic trigger (MODIFY two shipped Actions)** (PRD § 4.8 / § 9.1; AC-K-FSM-3, AC-K-J-7) — `RequireKyc` now **auto-places** a Customer-scope `kyc` Hold when KYC enters `pending`; `RecordKycVerified` now **auto-lifts** the active `kyc` Hold(s) when KYC reaches `verified` (the system auto-lift the discipline permits). `RecordKycRejected` leaves the Hold in place (§ 9.1 — *"rejected: the `kyc` Hold remains; Compliance reviews case-by-case"*). This is within-module Action orchestration (the KYC Action calls the Hold place/lift) exactly as `RequireKyc`'s shipped docblock anticipated — KYC fires no event, so the coupling cannot be event-driven.
- **Two Hold events** (verbatim PRD § 15.1; AC-K-EVT-2) — `CustomerHoldPlaced` and `CustomerHoldLifted`, each recorded transactionally with a **PII-free** payload (`hold_id`, `hold_type`, `scope_type`, `scope_id`, and the business `reason`). The envelope carries the actor + moment (AC-K-FSM-10). These are the only Hold events the § 15.1 catalog names; they are recorded for **all** scopes with `entity_type = 'Hold'` and the scope in the payload (the § 15.1 catalog names no `Profile`/`Account` Hold-event variant — using the canonical Customer-named events + a scope field is the zero-invention reading of AC-K-FSM-10's *"or Profile/Account analogs"*).
- **The uniform read-API tuple** (PRD § 4.8.1 / DEC-181; AC-K-XM-3 / XM-12) — a small read **contract** (interface + a PII-free DTO + a bound implementation) answering *"is this scope clear to transact?"* by returning the `(sanctions_status, active-Hold-list)` tuple, cascade-resolved. This is the single path every downstream transaction-initiation surface (Module S / C / E) will call. **It is exposed ready; the downstream consumers are NOT in this slice** (those modules don't exist yet — Phase 3+).
- **One additive migration** — the new `parties_holds` table (Postgres-truthful, SQLite-compatible; driver-guarded `CHECK`s deriving from `Enum::cases()`, mirroring the spine migrations). New `HoldType`, `HoldScope`, `HoldStatus` enums. New `Hold` model + factory. New `IllegalHoldLift` exception + localized copy in `lang/en/parties.php`.
- **Docs** — extend `CONTEXT.md` with the resolved Hold terms (the six types, the three scopes + cascade, the per-type lift discipline, the read-API tuple) and a Parties Hold contract note documenting the two event payloads + the read contract + the deferred seams below.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities

_None._ This change extends the existing `party-registry` capability with the Hold registry; it introduces no new capability (the Module-K-is-one-capability pattern the prior slices established).

### Modified Capabilities

- `party-registry`: gains the unified Hold registry, and the KYC lifecycle gains its `kyc`-Hold coupling. **ADDED** — *Hold Registry* (entity + six types + three scopes + cascade), *Hold Lifecycle and Lift Discipline* (place/lift Actions + the per-type discipline), *Hold Events* (`CustomerHoldPlaced`/`CustomerHoldLifted`), *Compliance Read-API* (the `(sanctions_status, active-Hold-list)` tuple). **MODIFIED** — *Customer KYC Lifecycle* (the `kyc` Hold is now auto-placed on `pending` and auto-lifted on `verified`; the "places no Hold" deferral is closed); *Birth States Recorded, Lifecycle Transitions Deferred* (narrowed — the Hold registry + the `kyc` coupling are now implemented, while the demand-side **status** FSMs, the Originating-Club lock, and the segment derivation remain deferred seams).

## Impact

- **New code** — `app/Modules/Parties/Enums/{HoldType,HoldScope,HoldStatus}.php`; `app/Modules/Parties/Models/Hold.php` + `database/factories/Parties/HoldFactory.php`; `app/Modules/Parties/Actions/{PlaceHold,LiftHold}.php`; `app/Modules/Parties/Events/{CustomerHoldPlaced,CustomerHoldLifted}.php`; `app/Modules/Parties/Exceptions/IllegalHoldLift.php` + a `hold` group in `lang/en/parties.php`; the read contract `app/Modules/Parties/Contracts/{PartyComplianceStatusReader.php, ComplianceStatus.php}` + its implementation + a `PartiesServiceProvider` binding; one additive migration `database/migrations/2026_06_18_000001_create_parties_holds_table.php`; tests under `tests/Feature/Modules/Parties/` and `tests/Unit/Modules/Parties/`.
- **Modified code (the coupling)** — `app/Modules/Parties/Actions/{RequireKyc,RecordKycVerified}.php` gain the `kyc`-Hold place/lift (each now injects the recorder/actor and orchestrates the Hold); `tests/Feature/Modules/Parties/CustomerKycLifecycleTest.php` flips from *"places no `kyc` Hold"* to asserting `CustomerHoldPlaced` on require and `CustomerHoldLifted` on verify. The KYC `kyc_status` writes themselves are unchanged.
- **One migration, no new dependency** — `git diff main -- composer.json composer.lock` stays empty; the only schema change is the **new additive** `parties_holds` table + its `CHECK`s (no change to existing tables — the `kyc` coupling reads/writes Holds, it adds no Customer column).
- **Reuses, does not modify** — `DomainEventRecorder` (incl. the open-transaction guard + `DomainEvent` return), `ActorContext`/`ActorRole`, the `Module` enum (`Module::Parties->value`), the spine Models, and the migration `CHECK`-from-`Enum::cases()` idiom.
- **Boundary & arch** — every reference is within Module K (`ModuleBoundariesTest`); the new `Hold` model follows the module-table-prefix convention (`parties_holds`) so `ModulePersistenceConventionsTest` passes. The **read contract is the one surface other modules will import** — it returns a PII-free DTO (never the `Hold` Eloquent model), preserving the "events + small read contracts, no model leak" boundary law.
- **DB engines** — the table, the place/lift transitions, the Hold events and the read-API cascade query all touch the database, so every DB-touching task is verified on **PostgreSQL 17** before close, in addition to SQLite (`knowledge/testing/rules.md`); the value-set `CHECK`s are driver-aware (Postgres-truthful, SQLite-compatible).
- **Invariant clarification** — root `CLAUDE.md` Invariant #7 (*"Holds are never auto-lifted"*) is over-broad vs the spec (DEC-160 / AC-K-FSM-11). ADR `2026-06-18-hold-lift-discipline-per-type.md` records the per-type discipline and recommends a reword; the `CLAUDE.md` edit is the human's (Protected file). The build follows the ADR.
- **Deliberate traceability gaps** — each deferred concern above maps to a named future change. The downstream enforcement surfaces (AC-K-J-20, AC-K-XM-3..11) and the finance-driven Hold triggers (AC-K-EVT-17/18/19) are recorded as Module S/C/E seams; the read-API they will consume ships ready.
