# Proposal — catalog-lifecycle-approval

## Why

This is the **lifecycle slice of Module 0 (Catalog)** and the direct follow-on to the archived `catalog-product-spine`. The spine stored every spine entity's full four-state domain as a `lifecycle_state` enum column but **wrote no transition** (`product-catalog` Requirement: "Lifecycle State Recorded, Transitions Deferred" — *"those behaviours are deferred to the `catalog-lifecycle-approval` change"*). It deferred, by name, five behaviours into this change: the **state transitions**, the **Creator → Reviewer → Approver approval workflow**, **rejection handling**, the **activation/retirement cascades**, and the **Producer-activation gate**.

It is unblocked **now** because the just-archived `parties-producer-lifecycle` emits `ProducerActivated` / `ProducerRetired` (the supply-side lifecycle events) — the exact events Module 0 consumes to gate Product Master activation. Wiring the consumer realizes **AC-K-XM-2** and closes the `producer_id` seam the spine left open (`catalog_product_masters` migration docblock: *"Producer validity … is a deferred lifecycle-gate concern (it consumes `ProducerActivated`)"*). This change is also the codebase's **first real cross-module domain-event consumer** — the delivery-ledger substrate exists (`event-substrate`), but nothing has registered a module consumer against it yet; this slice establishes that pattern (a Catalog-owned read-model projection, never a cross-module query — invariant 10).

It steps through **no open ADR gate**: domain events deliver **inline** at launch (`decisions/2026-06-12-event-substrate-and-audit-store.md`), so the consumer registers in `inline` mode (no `queued` consumer, no queue-driver ADR), and every cascade is orchestrated synchronously within the emitting transaction. No new architectural decision is required.

## What Changes

- **The four-state lifecycle FSM (`draft → reviewed → active → retired`)** is implemented **uniformly across all seven spine entities** (Product Master, Product Variant, Product Reference, Format, Case Configuration, Sellable SKU, Composite SKU; Module_0_PRD § 4.1, BR-Lifecycle-2). Transitions are explicit operator Actions that are the **sole writers** of `lifecycle_state` (mirroring the spine's `Create*` seam):
  - **submit for review** (`draft → reviewed`) — audit-recorded, **no** cross-module event (§ 4.2, AC-0-FSM-8);
  - **activate** (`reviewed → active`) — records the entity's verbatim **`*Activated`** event, subject to the activation gate (below);
  - **retire** (`active → retired`) — records **`*Retired`**;
  - **reopen for re-activation** (`retired → reviewed`) — audit-recorded, no event; re-activation flows back through `reviewed → active`, re-checking the gate (AC-0-J-10, BR-Lifecycle-2).
  - Every transition is **from-state guarded** against a transaction-locked re-read, rejecting an out-of-state call with a localized `IllegalLifecycleTransition`.
- **The Creator → Reviewer → Approver approval governance** (§ 4.2, BR-Lifecycle-1) is enforced at every commercial-impact transition: the actors that run **must be distinct people — self-approval is never allowed** (the separation-of-duties floor); the **role count is operational configuration** (`feedback_prd_rr_approval` — the 3-step default or a lighter 2-step Creator → Approver), with the floor holding at any configured depth; every step is recorded in the audit trail with actor identity, timestamp and decision. **Rejection handling** (§ 4.3, BR-Lifecycle-6): a rejected entity stays in `reviewed` with the rejection recorded (notes, actor, decision); the Creator edits in place — **no revert to `draft`** — and re-submits, restarting the flow; the full rejection history is preserved in the (append-only) audit trail.
- **The Producer activation gate** (§ 5.4, BR-Producer-1, AC-0-FSM-12): a **Product Master** cannot transition to `active` unless its linked Producer is `active` — a **hard gate** rejected at the workflow level at the moment of transition (and re-checked at re-activation, AC-0-J-10). The PRD's *"and KYC-verified"* conjunct is satisfied **transitively upstream**: the KYC four-state lifecycle is owned by `parties-compliance` (DEC-071 — KYC fields nullable, added additively), which tightens **`ActivateProducer`** so a Producer cannot reach `active` without a clear KYC verdict; Module 0 therefore gates on **producer-`active`** and inherits the KYC tightening with **no future change to its own gate** (a documented seam — see design D6).
- **Event consumption + a Catalog-owned producer-state projection** (§ 14.5, AC-0-EVT-20/21, AC-K-XM-2): a registered `DomainEventConsumer` consumes **`ProducerActivated`** (→ projects the producer `active`, **enabling** Master activation against it) and **`ProducerRetired`** (→ projects `retired`, **blocking new** Master activations under it). The consumer is idempotent and order-tolerant (per-producer `last_event_id` watermark, the substrate's documented latest-wins pattern). Consuming `ProducerRetired` **never** touches existing `active` Product Masters — **block-new, never cascade-retire** (§ 5.4 KYC-revocation symmetry, § 13.4 BR-Producer-2, § 14.5, AC-0-FSM-13, AC-0-EVT-21, AC-K-BR-Producer-4); and consuming `ProducerActivated` **never auto-activates** a queued Master (AC-0-BR-BulkImport-4 — re-submission is operator-initiated). The projection (`catalog_producer_states`) is the **one net-new migration**; the gate reads it, never Module K's tables (invariant 10).
- **The activation cascade — the parent-before-child hard gate** (§ 4.4, BR-Lifecycle-3, AC-0-FSM-10): a child cannot activate while its parent is not `active`. Per entity: a **Variant** requires its **Master** `active`; a **Product Reference** requires its **Variant** `active` **and** its **Format** `active`; a **Sellable SKU** requires its **PR** `active` **and** its **Case Configuration** `active`; a **Composite SKU** requires **every** constituent PR `active`. Standalone reference entities (**Format**, **Case Configuration**) activate independently. All parent reads are **within-module** (the only cross-module link is the Master → Producer projection).
- **The retirement cascade + reference integrity** (§ 4.5, § 4.7, BR-Lifecycle-4/5, AC-0-FSM-11): retiring a parent **preserves** existing active children for their current lifecycle (no retroactive invalidation) and only **blocks new** activation under the retired parent. An **operator-driven cascade** retires a Master plus its descendants in **parent-before-child** order (Master → Variants → PRs → SKUs; § 4.7, § 14.3). A single-entity retirement is **blocked while the entity has active within-catalog references** (BR-Lifecycle-5); the cross-module downstream-reference leg (Allocations/vouchers/Offers) is a **deferred seam** (those referencers arrive in Phase 3).
- **Fourteen `*Activated` / `*Retired` event classes** (verbatim Module_0_PRD § 14.1, category-neutral per § 18) — seven entities × {`*Activated`, `*Retired`} — each a `final` class (`NAME` / `ENTITY_TYPE` / static PII-free `payload()`), recorded through the platform `DomainEventRecorder` inside the transition's transaction; in a cascade, recorded **parent-before-child** (§ 14.3). The `draft → reviewed` checkpoint records **no** domain event (§ 14.2, AC-0-FSM-8).
- **One migration** — `catalog_producer_states` (the producer-state projection + watermark). **No** migration for any `lifecycle_state` column: all seven ship from the spine with their driver-guarded `CHECK`s. New `lang/en/catalog.php` groups for the transition/gate/governance exceptions (no hardcoded copy — invariant 12).
- **Docs**: extend `CONTEXT.md` with the resolved lifecycle/approval terms (review, activate, retire, reopen, the producer-state projection, the separation-of-duties floor) and a Catalog contract note documenting the consumed events + the fourteen emitted lifecycle event payloads and the two deferred seams (KYC-via-upstream; cross-module retirement-blocking references).

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities

_None._ This change extends the existing `product-catalog` capability with lifecycle, approval, gating and event-consumption behaviour; it introduces no new capability.

### Modified Capabilities

- `product-catalog`: the spine entities gain their lifecycle behaviour. **ADDED** — *Product Lifecycle State Machine* (the uniform four-state FSM + guards), *Approval Governance* (Creator → Reviewer → Approver, separation-of-duties, rejection, role-count), *Producer Activation Gate* (Master activation gated on linked-Producer `active`; KYC via upstream seam; re-activation re-checks), *Producer-State Projection and Event Consumption* (consume `ProducerActivated`/`ProducerRetired` into a Catalog read model; enable / block-new; preserve actives; no auto-activate), *Activation Cascade* (the parent-before-child hard gate per entity), *Retirement Cascade and Reference Integrity* (operator-driven parent-before-child retirement; preserve-existing; retirement blocked by active within-catalog references), *Product Lifecycle Events* (the fourteen verbatim `*Activated`/`*Retired` events; `draft → reviewed` event-silent). **MODIFIED** — *Lifecycle State Recorded, Transitions Deferred*: narrowed so the transitions, approval workflow, rejection handling, cascades and Producer gate are **no longer deferred** (they are implemented by the ADDED requirements), while the cross-module reference-integrity and KYC legs remain documented seams.

## Impact

- **New code** — `app/Modules/Catalog/Models/ProducerState.php` (the projection model); `app/Modules/Catalog/Consumers/ProducerLifecycleProjector.php` (the first cross-module `DomainEventConsumer`), registered in `CatalogServiceProvider::boot()` via the injected `ConsumerRegistry`; a shared lifecycle mechanism (the transition map + from-state guard + separation-of-duties governance + audit recording) under `app/Modules/Catalog/Lifecycle/` (or equivalent); per-entity transition Actions under `app/Modules/Catalog/Actions/`; fourteen event classes under `app/Modules/Catalog/Events/`; transition/gate/governance exceptions under `app/Modules/Catalog/Exceptions/` + localized copy in `lang/en/catalog.php`; one migration `database/migrations/*_create_catalog_producer_states_table.php`; tests under `tests/Feature/Modules/Catalog/` and `tests/Unit/Modules/Catalog/`.
- **One migration, no new dependency** — `git diff main -- composer.json composer.lock` stays empty; the only schema addition is the additive `catalog_producer_states` projection (every `lifecycle_state` column + its `CHECK` ships from the spine).
- **Reuses, does not modify** — `DomainEventRecorder` (incl. `causationId`/`correlationId` + the `DomainEvent` return), `AuditRecorder`, `ActorContext`/`ActorRole`, `ConsumerRegistry` + `InlineDeliveryExecutor` + the scheduled `events:sweep`, the `Module` enum (`Module::Catalog->value`), the `LifecycleState` enum (all four cases ship from the spine), and the seven spine Models (which stay persistence-only — the transition Action is the sole `lifecycle_state` writer, preserving the immutability discipline for identity columns). The consumer reads `$event->payload['producer_id']` only — **no `App\Modules\Parties\*` import** (invariant 10; enforced by `ModuleBoundariesTest`).
- **First-consumer establishment** — registering a module consumer is net-new wiring; `CatalogServiceProvider::boot()` (currently an empty stub) gains the `ConsumerRegistry::register('ProducerActivated'|'ProducerRetired', ProducerLifecycleProjector::class)` calls. The delivery-ledger / sweep substrate is unchanged.
- **DB engines** — the projection, the transitions and their events touch the database, so every DB-touching task is verified on **PostgreSQL 17** before close, in addition to SQLite (`knowledge/testing/rules.md`); the consumer-delivery tests use `DatabaseMigrations` (not `RefreshDatabase`) so the recorder's post-commit inline hook fires at `transactionLevel 0`.
- **Deliberate traceability gaps** — each deferred concern above maps to a named future change. Two spec-faithful seams are recorded explicitly: **the KYC conjunct of the gate is enforced upstream** (`parties-compliance` tightens `ActivateProducer`, not Module 0), and **BR-Lifecycle-5's cross-module reference leg** is enforced over within-catalog references only until the Phase-3 referencers exist.
