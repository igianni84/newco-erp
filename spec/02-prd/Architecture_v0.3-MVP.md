## §1 Objectives and Architectural Design Principles (launch scope)

### §1.1 Objectives of the NewCo launch ERP

NewCo's launch ERP must:

- Establish a single authoritative system of record across NewCo's commerce, custody, fulfilment, and finance domains — for the **Lean launch MVP** (GOV-1: KEEP only what the core loop or the compliance/data-integrity floor requires; defer the rest, with a seam).
- Correctly model the business abstractions of the producer-club aggregator pattern (Customer, Profile, Producer, Supplier, Club, ProducerAgreement, Allocation, Offer, Voucher, Order, the physical bottle, Shipping Order, financial events) — under the generalised, category-neutral **Product** spine (Module 0; sole launch `Product Type` = `WINE`).
- Support the launch commercial models without fragmentation — **passive consignment V1 + V2** (the default), under a single Allocation entity. **Direct Purchase is deferred at launch** (confirmed: no launch deal — Phase C item I/Q4); its `direct_purchase` enum/FSM/discriminators idle-but-seamed across A/D/B/E/S (re-enable additive).
- Operate as the operational brain: the ERP decides commercial policy, allocation commitments, eligibility, fulfilment routing, and financial-event recording; external systems execute authorised actions and report outcomes back; **operators drive every producer/back-office write through the Admin Panel** (P2 — manual-first where safe), the one platform-wide producer write being Module K membership approve/decline.

### §1.2 Architectural Design Principles (product level)

Each principle binds across multiple modules and is therefore a cross-cutting concern no per-module PRD owns end-to-end. Carried from v0.2 §1.2 and refreshed for the launch re-baseline.

**ERP as authority, not interface.** The ERP owns commercial policy, pricing, eligibility, allocations, custody, fulfilment routing, and financial events. Customer-facing surfaces (Consumer Portal, Producer Portal, Bottle Page), the **Admin Panel**, Logilize WMS, Avalanche on-chain, Airwallex, Xero, HubSpot are execution arms. Every business state change either originates in the ERP or is validated by it before it commits. **Inventory authority within the ERP lives in Module B** (InboundBatch, StockPosition, Case, QuarantineRecord, Stocktake; ATP source per allocation; the `received_to_date` per-allocation receipt-position read — MVP-DEC-037; receiving physical-match authority; stocktake + adjustment authority; provenance immutability; no-overselling at the physical-inventory layer; committed-inventory protection); Logilize remains the physical-execution arm (sub-warehouse / pick-pack-dispatch) and does not concentrate ERP-side inventory-source-of-truth.

**Four-way reconciliation discipline as a first-order design concern.** NewCo's launch ERP rests on four orthogonal authorities each owning a distinct slice of bottle / inventory / commercial / financial state, reconciled through explicit cross-module event flows rather than silent overrides: **Logilize** = physical-execution authority; **Module B** = ERP-side inventory-ledger authority; **Module S** = commercial-state authority (Allocation visibility read + Offer + Cart Hold + Order/Voucher FSM + customer-facing INV1/INV2/INV3 issuance); **Module E** = financial-state authority (payment execution + supplier settlement + chargeback + non-revenue cost + Xero routing + multi-currency dual recording). Reconciliation flows through three primitives — **receiving** (Module D = documents / Module B = physical match per DEC-194; `InboundBatchDiscrepancy` reopens the InboundEvent into DISCREPANCY), **stocktake** (Module B Stocktake + 4-state lifecycle), **adjustment** (Module B proposal-and-confirmation with `InventoryAdjusted` + `InventoryShortfallDetected`) — plus the **QuarantineRecord gatekeeper** (Module B never creates inventory records from unverified Logilize data; resolved QuarantineRecords are immutable). The day-to-day load-bearing application is the **two-layer no-overselling guard** (§8.2 chain 1). **At launch the Stage-8 *workflow automation* is manual-first (D16 / N1) — the integrity core, the entities, the events, and the DEC-194 split are FLOOR-kept; only the automated reciprocal round-trips defer** (§5.5).

**Explicit domain modelling.** Every operationally-meaningful concept is a first-class entity with its own identity, lifecycle, and state machine — Allocation, Offer, Voucher (7-state at launch — GIFTED deferred with D5), Order, SerializedBottle, InboundBatch, StockPosition, Case, QuarantineRecord, Stocktake, Shipping Order, Customer/Profile/Hold/ProducerAgreement, ProcurementIntent/PurchaseOrder/InboundEvent, **Product Master / Product Variant / Product Reference** / Intrinsic SKU / Composite SKU (Module 0, generalised). Each is documented in its owning module's PRD (§2.2).

**Three-actor split for invoicing (LOAD-BEARING — DEC-119).** The customer-facing invoice lifecycle is split across three actors: **Module S = the EVENT** (decides WHEN an invoice fires — INV1/INV2/INV3 issuance lifecycle, Voucher transitions, the storage clock — all Module-S-internal per R2); **Xero = the ARTIFACT** (generates WHAT the customer and tax authority receive — PDF, numbering, legal text, jurisdiction compliance); **Module E = the PAYMENT + the ACCOUNTING RECORD + the Xero ROUTING LAYER** (the HOW — executes the Airwallex charge, routes the financial-event payload to Xero for accounting per DEC-072, routes the document-generation request to Xero). **This split is the most architecturally load-bearing element of the ERP, and it is symmetric on the supplier side: Module E is the payment executor on both sides — it emits `AirwallexChargeExecuted` on the customer charge and `SupplierPaymentCompleted` on supplier-payment clearing (R4 — §3.6).**

**Distributed responsibility for Holds.** Holds are not owned by a single module. Module E decides when a payment-driven Hold trigger fires; **Module K records the Hold entity and manages its lifecycle** (authoritative for existence, scope, reason, lift conditions); Module S, Module C, and Module E read the Hold set at the surfaces where it bites (DEC-181 uniformity — every transaction-initiation surface re-reads sanctions + Hold at the moment of action). **Module K's Hold registry is trigger-agnostic (N2):** at launch the **chargeback trigger is automated** (D21 KEPT — Paolo override; `CustomerChargebackFlagged` from Module E's automated Airwallex chain) while the **storage-payment trigger is manual-first** (D4 deferred; `StoragePaymentFailed`/`StoragePaymentSucceeded` from Module E's manual-first path). The Hold's lift requires explicit Module-K-side action (no auto-lift on `admin`/`fraud`/`compliance`/`credit`/`CHARGEBACK_REVIEW`; `STORAGE_PAYMENT_FAILED` lifts per-cycle on `StoragePaymentSucceeded`).

**Late binding for serialized inventory; batch-quantity dispatch for non-serialized.** Customer rights (Vouchers) stay abstract; physical bottles stay fungible within their Allocation pool; binding a SerializedBottle to a Voucher happens only at fulfilment (Module C late-binding selection — **D13 simplified to FIFO + manual tiebreak** at launch; the bottle-side warehouse-efficiency optimisation defers). For non-serialized inventory (the universal fallback under the D12 decouple), late binding does not apply: Module C records an allocation + InboundBatch + quantity tuple at dispatch (`BottleShippedAsNonSerialized`). Allocation lineage is preserved at batch granularity either way.

**Admin-first, self-serve-later — the Admin Panel as a first-class operator surface (P2 + DEC-083/DEC-115).** DEC-083 admin-parity (extended to Offer-level ops by DEC-115) is a **backend contract** — every per-Allocation and Club-Offer operation is exposable from both the Producer Portal and the Admin Panel, each emitted event carrying an `actor_role: producer | newco_ops` tag. **At launch the Admin-Panel side is exercised (`newco_ops`); producer-facing write UIs are deferred and build post-launch on the same backend (P1 seam) — no backend capability is cut.** The single retained producer write platform-wide is **Module K membership approve/decline**; the consumer storefront/cellar/Bottle Page are exempt (self-serve KEPT); the Producer Portal read + full 7-section reporting (D23) is KEPT. The manual-first defers made the Admin Panel *more* load-bearing (D24) — it is recorded here as a **first-class cross-cutting surface** (§2.3), correcting v1.1's implicit treatment.

**Financial-event immutability.** Every financial event recorded by Module E is immutable post-emission; corrections flow through compensating events (refund, credit note via Xero, `NonRevenueCostRecorded` wrapper), never edits. Post-sync immutability is FLOOR (corrections via credit notes only). The audit chain is unbroken; allocation lineage on every financial event preserves supply-side traceability to settlement.

**Multi-currency dual-recording (D18 — FLOOR).** Every customer-facing financial event is recorded in **both** the customer-facing currency and EUR base, with per-leg FX rate-lock; **refunds use the original captured rate**; `FXVarianceRecorded` reconciles the Airwallex-actual-vs-snapshot gap. The 5 launch currencies (EUR base + USD + GBP + CHF + JPY) all admit the same pattern. **KEPT WHOLE — not a candidate** (the machinery is fixed-cost; narrowing currencies saves ~nothing).

**i18n-ready by design.** The data model carries translatable strings per-attribute (i18n-keyed JSON); adding a locale post-launch is configuration, not migration. Launch locales: Bottle Page + Consumer Portal six (EN+IT+FR+DE+JP+ZH); Producer Portal EN+IT; Admin Panel EN+IT. (Infra reportedly built; content may stagger — D2 KEEP.)

**Allocation lineage as a cross-cutting principle.** The Allocation is the unbroken thread from supply commitment to fulfilment to financial settlement: every Voucher (S) traces to one Allocation (A); every ProcurementIntent/PurchaseOrder (D) traces to one Allocation; every SerializedBottle (B) and Bottle Page render carries the source Allocation; every Shipping Order line (C) reads its source Allocation pool at late binding; every financial event (E) carries the allocation lineage — including the **per-constituent `commercial_terms` `C_i`** lineage the deferred OC 5% + settlement engine reads (the capture is whole at launch; only the computation defers — D19).

**Domain events as cross-module contracts.** All cross-module communication flows through versioned domain events — explicit, audited contracts. This document and the per-module event catalogues commit to event **names**, **business signals**, and **cross-module contract semantics**; the wire-level shape (transport, serialisation, broker semantics, ordering, idempotency) is dev-team scope (DEC-073). The system-level launch event contract is §8.1.

**Defer without burning bridges; manual-first where safe (P1 + GOV-1 — the launch-specific principle).** Every deferred/simplified capability names the **seam** that makes the post-launch build *additive* and points to the roadmap (#12). The manual-first operator surfaces (settlement composition, INV3 dunning, Stage-8 inventory workflows, returns/replacement FSM, white-glove quotes) **record the same data their future automated engines consume** — the recording / integrity-core is whole at launch; the automation is the roadmap. Phase C verified every seam is real on both sides.

## §3 Cross-Module Canonical Business Flows (launch)

The load-bearing cross-module flows as numbered product-step narratives, re-baselined to the launch scope and **landing the four RECONCILEs + the editorial notes at the flow level.** Per DEC-074 the per-module event catalogues are the authoritative event-by-event sources; these flows are the cross-module product narrative. (Names per §2.4.)

### §3.1 B2C Voucher Purchase — Card Payment (the canonical flow)

1. **Catalog identity established (Module 0).** Product Master + Product Variant + **Product Reference** + Intrinsic SKU activated through the 4-state lifecycle + 3-step approval; Module 0 emits `Product*Activated`.
2. **Allocation registered + activated (Module A).** Operator creates the Allocation against the PR; sets visibility, sub-pool partition, `commercial_terms`, counterparty FKs; **Allocation transitions DRAFT → ACTIVE on operator-publish post-PO-commit, uniformly (DEC-183 / R1)** — `AllocationActivated`. (No `direct_purchase` allocations at launch — item I.)
3. **Offer published (Module S).** Allocation Operator (Admin Panel at launch — producer write UI deferred, L-PP) creates the Offer referencing the Allocation; 5-rule publication validation gates `OfferActivated`.
4. **Customer browses + adds to Cart (Module S; Module K reads).** Cart-add reads Customer Segment + Profile state + Hold set + Originating Club via Module K; cart-add places no stock reservation.
5. **Checkout — Cart Hold (Module S → Module A).** Module S places a strict-timer Cart Hold on each line's Allocation; the **two-layer no-overselling guard** validates at hold placement — **Module A Layer 1 (`qty − issued ≥ 0`) ∧ the storefront lesser-of read of Module B Layer-2 physical ATP, per sub-pool** (§8.2 chain 1). *(Layer-2's sale-gate is sourcing-model-scoped — `passive_v1` lines gate on Layer 1 alone; §8.2 chain 1 / MVP-DEC-027.)*
6. **Stacking + Club Credit (Module S).** Mutual-exclusivity matrix; Club Credit auto-applies at checkout-render (carry-forward KEPT — K.17).
7. **Sanctions / Hold gate (Module S reads Module K — THE consumer-side enforcement point, DEC-113).** Pre-payment-authorization, Module S reads Module K's `(sanctions_status, active-Hold-list)` tuple; a non-`passed` status or any active Hold blocks the order (`OrderBlockedBySanctionsGate` / `OrderBlockedByHoldGate`). Module K and Module A are sanctions-blind by design.
8. **Hero Package three-gate eligibility** (if `is_hero_package`; Module S reads Module K — Profile state + single-per-year + the Capacity Invariant against Module A `qty`, counting the **seat-occupying set `Active` + `Suspended`** — MVP-DEC-017). **The Hero Package *joining* charge is not this consumer card flow:** it is **committed at application** (payment method + charge-on-approval mandate) and **captured at producer approval — approval = charge = activation, atomically** — against the mandate (Module K §4.2.1 / Module S §5; MVP-DEC-016). A charge that fails at approval consumes no seat. Renewals capture on the auto-renew cycle.
9. **Card authorise+capture (Module S → Module E → Airwallex).** Module E creates the Payment record; the Airwallex integration authorises+captures in one step; **Module E emits `AirwallexChargeExecuted`** (the customer-side payment-execution event — symmetric with the supplier-side `SupplierPaymentCompleted`, §3.6).
10. **Order CONFIRMED + Voucher ISSUED + INV1 (Module S).** Module S transitions Order → CONFIRMED + Voucher → ISSUED; emits **`InvoiceINV1Issued`** (INV1 = bottle-amount-only; no excise/VAT — MPV regime). The Cart Hold converts to the Voucher; the binding to the Allocation is now immutable.
11. **OC 5% accrual (Module S, Discovery only).** `DiscoveryRevenueShareAccrued` fires at INV1 = post-payment-cleared, on headline `P_d`, reading Module K's `OriginatingClubLocked` + Module A's lineage (the one-shot capture — §8.2). Module E records it as `OCShareAccrued`; **the 5% computation + settlement defer with the engine (D19) — the capture is whole at launch.**
12. **Xero routing (Module E → Xero).** Module E consumes `InvoiceINV1Issued`; routes the financial-event payload + document-generation request to Xero (DEC-072/028); Xero generates the INV1 PDF; Module E confirms the document URL back to Module S.
13. **Procurement intent fires (Module D, V1/V2).** `VoucherIssued` (Module S) is the trigger for Module D PI auto-fire; PI accumulates into a PO at the producer-settlement cadence. **`VoucherIssued` is also the sell-through signal driving Module D's PO-level title PRODUCER→NEWCO transition (item F — there is no separate `SellThroughRecorded`).**
14. **Customer holds Voucher in Cellar.** Cellar render composes from the six-module read (Module S Voucher state + storage accrual; Module C SO state + in-transit display; Module B SerializedBottle / Bottle Page link + warehouse-level inventory summary; Module 0 PR / Product Variant content).

### §3.2 Bank-Transfer Flow — INV1 with PENDING_PAYMENT Pre-State

Diverges from §3.1 between steps 5 and 10. Cart Hold extends to the SEPA/SWIFT clearing window; Order persists in PENDING_PAYMENT (no proforma document — no INV-P at launch). Airwallex notifies Module E asynchronously when funds clear; **Module E emits `BankTransferFundsCleared` to Module S**, which transitions Order → CONFIRMED + Voucher → ISSUED + emits `InvoiceINV1Issued`. From there identical to §3.1 step 12. If the window expires without funds-cleared, Module S auto-VOIDs the Voucher (`VoucherVoided`); the Cart Hold releases; Allocation capacity is restored. (The bank-transfer operator-fallback reconciliation — when the webhook fails or no auto-match — is a finance-ops console surface, §2.3 / §5.6.)

### §3.3 Redemption → Shipment → INV2

1. **Customer requests redemption (Module S → Module C).** `VoucherRedemptionRequested`; Module C creates the Shipping Order in `draft`. All SOs are customer-initiated at launch.
2. **SO eligibility + destination + shipment gate (Module C; Module K + Module 0 + Module D reads).** Compliance Hold + sanctions/**OFAC at all destinations** → `compliance_hold` sub-state if blocked (DEC-181); destination eligibility two-tier — **the automated pre-cleared Tier-1 list is narrowed to low-friction destinations at launch; complex/high-excise/US-state destinations route via the white-glove manual quote (D3, §2.3)**; the **shipment gate** awaits Module D's `InboundEventPhysicallyAccepted` (DEC-081 — decoupled from sellability). The **in-transit redemption-block is FLOOR** (cannot redeem stock not physically at Vinlock); basic in-transit display ("in transit; ETA X", admin-estimate — carrier-ETA-precision is the D17 defer).
3. **Carrier selection + shipping-fee quote (Module C).** Auto rule-driven or **manual operator-entry (`quote_origin = manual` — the white-glove enabler)**; `ShippingFeeQuoted` (informational on INV1).
4. **Late binding (Module C; Module A + Module B reads).** **FIFO by Voucher expiry + manual tiebreak (D13 simplified)**; **no-oversell-at-pick reads Module B StockPosition** (§8.2 chain 1); within the source Allocation's pool boundary; respects the effective-breakability rule. For non-serialized Vouchers: batch dispatch (allocation + InboundBatch + quantity tuple). `BottlePicked` records the bind.
5. **SO `planned → picking → shipped` (Module C; Logilize executes the 4 fulfilment streams — R3).** Module C sends the outbound pick instruction and consumes Streams 2–4 (pick-confirm / dispatch-confirm / delivery-confirm) + the customs-documentation-completed event; **storage-location is Module B's Stream B1 (R3 — Module C no longer reads storage-location directly from Logilize).** Module C emits `BottlePicked` then `ShipmentDispatched` (carrying actual shipping cost + `ExciseCalculated`). Pick-time discrepancies (`manual_review`) feed the shared Logilize discrepancy queue (B+C — §2.3).
6. **Voucher SHIPPED + INV2 (Module S consumes Module C `ShipmentDispatched`).** Module S transitions Voucher → SHIPPED; **Module S composes and issues `InvoiceINV2Issued`** — composing the actual shipping fee, destination VAT (MPV), the **excise line from Module C's `ExciseCalculated`** (Module C *contributes* excise + actual shipping; **Module S *composes + issues* INV2** — the precise division), and the mid-semester storage roll-in (Module-S-internal — R2). **The excise computation runs even in the white-glove flow** (the tax floor cannot be cut by manual routing).
7. **NFT burn (Module C → Module S → Module B) — rides the D12 decouple.** `ShipmentDispatched` → Module S `VoucherShipped` → Module B NFT burn. **At launch the on-chain layer is decoupled** (`nft_reference` nullable + back-fillable); for non-serialized stock the **universal-fallback** `BottleShippedAsNonSerialized` fires regardless. Module B does not subscribe to Module C events directly (Voucher-state authority stays at Module S).
8. **Xero routing + saved-card charge (Module E → Xero + Airwallex).** Module E consumes `InvoiceINV2Issued`; routes to Xero; charges the saved card (`AirwallexChargeExecuted`).
9. **Voucher CONSUMED (Module S, on Module C `BottleDelivered`).**

### §3.4 Storage Clock → Semi-Annual INV3 (Module-S-internal — R2)

1. **Storage clock starts (Module S, with the single Module D read — R2).** `storage_accrual_start_date = max(INV1_date + 12 months, InboundEventPhysicallyAccepted_date)`. **The one cross-module storage coordination at launch is the single Module D → Module S read of `InboundEventPhysicallyAccepted` (the warehouse anchor) — there is NO bidirectional Module S ↔ Module E contract at INV2 (R2 corrects the stale DEC-118 framing; storage is Module-S-internal per DEC-119).**
2. **Per-bottle accrual (Module S, monthly).** `StorageFeeAccrued` fires per bottle per month after the 12-month-free + bottle-at-warehouse double condition (€0.25/bottle/month). **Module-S-emitted** — not consumed from Module E.
3. **Semi-annual INV3 issuance (Module S).** End-June / end-December, Module S aggregates and emits **`InvoiceINV3Issued`** (Module-S-emitted per DEC-119).
4. **Xero routing + saved-card charge + dunning (Module E → Xero + Airwallex; Module K).** Module E consumes `InvoiceINV3Issued`; routes to Xero; charges the saved card. **On charge failure, the 3-stage escalation is MANUAL-FIRST at launch (D4):** the operator drives retry → places the K-Hold (`StoragePaymentFailed` → Module K `STORAGE_PAYMENT_FAILED` Hold) → drives Suspension if it persists. **The `StoragePaymentFailed` → K-Hold → Suspension event chain + the admin-configurable thresholds are the seam (the automated orchestration is the roadmap); the first storage cycle is months out** (the defer is timing-safe). **The sanctions/Hold re-read at charge (DEC-181) + Hold-no-auto-lift are FLOOR — never deferred.** (N2: the storage-payment Hold trigger is manual-first; Module K's registry is trigger-agnostic.)
5. **Mid-semester shipment carve-out.** When a Voucher ships mid-semester (§3.3), Module S rolls the unbilled in-semester storage months onto INV2 (Module-S-internal) instead of carrying to the next INV3 cycle.

### §3.5 14-Day Cancellation → Refund (D6 manual-first decisioning)

1. **Customer requests cancellation (Module S, within 14 days of INV1).** Pre-shipment only; the right is WAIVED once SHIPPED (EU Distance Contracts Directive Art. 16 — the legal floor KEPT).
2. **Voucher VOIDED (Module S).** `VoucherVoided`; Allocation capacity restored on the source Allocation (per-voucher partial cancellation).
3. **Refund decisioning (Module S — D6 manual-first).** At launch the operator **records the refund + cause** and offers store-credit-105% by judgment via the single `REFUND_COMPENSATION` coupon (the legal floor + the mechanism KEPT; the **automated refund-cost-matrix routing + producer-fault clawback netting defer** — the netting defers with the settlement engine, D19). The cause taxonomy + coupon + refund-event payloads are the seam.
4. **Refund executed (Module S → Module E → Airwallex).** Module S emits `RefundRequested`; **Module E executes the Airwallex refund + records `RefundExecuted`** (FX-correct — at the original captured rate, D18; credit-note discipline; routes to Xero). For Discovery, Module S also emits `DiscoveryRevenueShareReversed` (Module E records into the Section-D running balance).
5. **Post-shipment exception.** The rare supervisor-override post-delivery refund (`SupervisorOverridePostDeliveryRefund`; **multi-actor: initiator ≠ supervisor**) is a documented exception, not a routine path.

### §3.6 Supplier-Payment → Ownership Cascade (R4 — the E-emits contract)

> **This replaces the predecessor's §3.6 "Direct Purchase Activation Chain."** Direct Purchase is deferred at launch (item I); and per **R1**, `SupplierPaymentCompleted` has **no FSM-activation role** — Allocation activation is operator-publish post-PO-commit, uniformly (§3.1 step 2). The **DEC-194 receiving split** (Module D = documents / Module B = physical match) — on a physical-count variance Module B emits `InboundBatchDiscrepancy` back to Module D, which reopens the InboundEvent into DISCREPANCY (**manual-first round-trip at launch — N1, identical depth on both PRDs**).
3. **Supplier payment clears (Module E — the payment executor; R4).** **Module E emits `SupplierPaymentCompleted`** on supplier-payment clearing. At launch the operator records the manual supplier payment in Module E's finance surface (settlement is operator-run — D19 deferred); post-launch, Module E's settlement engine. Atomic per PO (partial PO settlement deferred — OQ-20). **This corrects the cut-sheets' "D-emits" reading** (the cut-sheets stay as the Phase B record): Module D has no independent trigger, and payment execution is Module E's (the three-actor split DEC-119 assigns PAYMENT to E; symmetric with the customer-side `AirwallexChargeExecuted`).
4. **Module D consumes → settle/close the PO.** Module D consumes `SupplierPaymentCompleted` → the PO CLOSED transition. **Module D's own procurement financial events stay D-emitted** (`InboundEventCostFinalized`, `DiscrepancyResolutionRecorded`, `ConsignmentReceiptRecorded`, `ReverseInboundEventRecorded`, `PurchaseOrderIssued`) — only the *payment-completion* event is E-emitted.
5. **Module B consumes → inventory `ownership_flag` PRODUCER → NEWCO.** Module B consumes the same E-emitted event independently → `OwnershipTransitioned` (the bottle becomes NewCo-owned because NewCo has paid). **The inventory `ownership_flag` ledger keys to the *payment* signal (`SupplierPaymentCompleted`) — distinct from the PO-level title ledger (Module D), which keys to the *sale/shipment* signal (`VoucherIssued`) — N3.** The supplier-EUR FX leg rate-locks at this moment (D18).
6. **Module E records + routes to Xero.** Module E records the supplier-side financial event; routes to Xero per `Allocation.sourcing_model`. **No accounting position (DEC-072) — Xero decides GL.**

*(The deferred Direct-Purchase activation chain — operator-initiated PI + at-PO-creation timing + the immediate-Xero/Section-E routing — idles across A/D/B/E/S with its enum/FSM/discriminators retained; re-enable is additive — §6.)*

### §5.1 Internationalisation (i18n)

### §5.2 Multi-Currency (D18 — FLOOR)

Customer-facing launch set: EUR (base) + USD + GBP + CHF + JPY. **Dual-currency on every customer-facing financial event** — Module E records each in both the customer-facing currency and EUR, FX applied at emission with **per-leg rate-lock** (three independent FX moments: customer-currency leg at charge; supplier-EUR leg at `SupplierPaymentCompleted`; OC leg at INV1). **Refunds use the original captured rate**; `FXVarianceRecorded` reconciles the Airwallex-actual-vs-snapshot gap. FX-buffer policy is admin-configurable. **KEPT WHOLE — not a candidate** (the dual-recording machinery is fixed-cost regardless of currency count — simplifying would break FX-correct refunds).

### §5.3 Audit Trail and Retention (+ the operator-surface arm)

Every critical action generates an immutable audit record (actor, action, timestamp, before/after, authorisation basis) across all eight modules. **The `actor_role` audit envelope is recorded on every operator action through the Admin Panel** (§2.3) — the operator-surface arm of the audit floor (`actor_role: newco_ops` on every back-office write; `actor_role: producer | newco_ops` on the one producer write under DEC-115 parity). **Retention** — 10-year (Italian invoice retention; EU norms): Xero retains the invoice-document layer; Module E retains the financial-event layer (post-sync immutability — corrections via credit notes only); per-module audit logs retain entity-state changes.
