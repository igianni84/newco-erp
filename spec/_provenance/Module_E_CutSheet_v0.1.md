## ¬ß1 Verdict & counts

**Module E is the finance terminus ‚Äî KEPT WHOLE on its customer-side payment-execution + Xero-routing + tax-correct-invoicing-recording floor + the three-actor split + the multi-currency dual-record (D18), with the #3-heaviest lever of the whole exercise landing in-module: D19 (DEFER the supplier-settlement *engine* to operator-run), compounded by D4 (DEFER the saved-card INV3 auto-escalation to manual-first), plus D20 (SDI already-deferred). D21 (chargeback ingestion) was drafted as a SIMPLIFY‚Üímanual but is KEPT at Paolo's ratification ‚Äî customer payment automation rides the day-1 Airwallex integration and is not a Lean cut (fact 4).** This is the genuinely cut-heavy finish the calibration predicted ‚Äî but the shape holds one last time: **defer the *automation/sophistication around* the floor; keep the floor whole.** The "pay" step (Clause 1 ‚Äî execute the Airwallex charge + route to Xero for accounting + document-generation + confirm the Xero-hosted URL back to Module S) and the data-integrity heart of FX-correct refunds (Clause 4 ‚Äî dual-record every financial event in customer-currency + EUR) stay non-negotiably whole; the cuts land on the *settlement engine* (Clause 2) and the *INV3 dunning orchestration* (the D4 arm of Clause 1) ‚Äî never on payment execution, never on chargeback/payment automation (D21 KEPT), never on tax-correct-invoicing recording, never on dual-record FX-correctness, never on the audit/credit-note/immutability discipline. Seven facts shape the verdict:

1. **D19 (supplier settlement automation ‚Äî the #3-heaviest lever overall) is the headline in-module DEFER, and it is genuinely heavy ‚Äî but cleanly deferrable because the first quarterly close lands months post-launch and the *recording* is the seam.** The quarterly settlement **engine** (Clause 2, ¬ß4) ‚Äî per-Producer/Supplier runs aggregating sell-through + OC share + Direct-Purchase events; the `ProducerSettlementStatementIssued` 5-section composition (DEC-156); the `SellThroughSettled` + `OCShareAccrued` aggregation; the DEC-161 settlement-counterparty disambiguation; the DEC-164/025 clawback netting (Section C); the producer settlement-currency option (¬ß4.6); the settlement-statement FSM; the Xero AP routing of statements ‚Äî is **DEFERRED to operator-run for the first cycle(s).** **Seam (P1):** Module E keeps **recording** every settlement-input event the deferred engine will aggregate ‚Äî it consumes Module D's ratified-KEPT `SupplierPaymentCompleted` (atomic per PO, D.24) + `InboundEventCostFinalized`, Module S's `DiscoveryRevenueShareAccrued`/`RefundIssued`/`DiscoveryRevenueShareReversed` stream, and the per-purchase OC-accrual capture ‚Äî and routes each to Xero in real time (¬ß7.1). At launch the operator composes the first statement(s) manually from the recorded events + runs Xero AP manually; the *engine* is built after the first close. **Honest:** the genuine risk is that the defer must not drop the *recording* (the seam) ‚Äî it does not: Module D ratified KEEP its recording (D.24/Q5), Module S ratified KEEP its emission (S.19), and Module E keeps consuming + recording. Partial PO settlement already deferred (OQ-20) ‚Äî carry. *(See ¬ß3.1; Q1.)*

2. **D18 (multi-currency dual-record) is the data-integrity heart of FX-correct refunds ‚Äî KEPT WHOLE; FLOOR; NOT a candidate.** Every customer-facing financial event recorded in customer currency **and** EUR; per-leg FX rate-lock at each leg's emission moment (customer leg at `PaymentCaptureSucceeded`/`BankTransferFundsCleared`, supplier leg at `SupplierPaymentCompleted`, OC leg at INV1 emission ‚Äî DEC-169 E6-48); EOD Rome snapshot + admin buffer + daily refresh + Airwallex mid-market; **refund at the original captured rate (no fresh snapshot at refund)**; `FXVarianceRecorded` for the Airwallex-vs-snapshot capture gap. **D1 keeps all 5 currencies (EUR/USD/GBP/CHF/JPY), so the dual-record machinery is kept whole ‚Äî narrowing the currency count saves ~nothing (the machinery is fixed-cost regardless of count).** Unsafe to simplify, and I say so. Not a candidate. *(See ¬ß3.4; Q4.)*

3. **D4 (saved-card auto-escalation) is a DEFER ‚Üí manual first cycle, with the same "first cycle is months out" logic as D19.** Card auth+capture (DEC-158) + bank-transfer PENDING_PAYMENT (DEC-159) + the saved-card charge for INV2 at shipment (DEC-158) are **core-loop FLOOR ‚Äî KEPT.** What defers is the **INV3-failed-charge 3-stage auto-escalation orchestration** (DEC-160 ‚Äî Stage 1 auto-retry window ‚Üí Stage 2 auto-emit `StoragePaymentFailed` ‚Üí K Hold ‚Üí Stage 3 auto-Profile-Suspension) + the automated multi-cycle composition (parallel chains, per-cycle Hold-lift, strongest-Suspension-wins). The first INV3 storage-billing cycle lands **months post-launch** (storage accrues only after the 12-month-free + bottle-at-warehouse double anchor; INV3 fires semi-annually end-Jun/end-Dec). **Seam (P1):** the INV3-failed-charge ‚Üí `StoragePaymentFailed` ‚Üí K Hold ‚Üí Profile Suspension **event chain** + the admin-configurable staged thresholds are retained; the *automated* retry/dunning orchestration is the defer (operator handles the first cycle(s) manually via the Admin Panel + manual K Hold placement). ### ¬ß3.1 D19 ‚Äî the supplier-settlement *engine* defer (the kickoff ¬ß9 headline; the #3 lever overall)

The kickoff nominated D19 as Module E's headline cut and "the #3-heaviest lever of the whole exercise," with the explicit mandate to "confirm the quarterly settlement engine can cleanly DEFER to operator-run first cycle(s) while Module E keeps RECORDING the settlement-input events ‚Äî verify the recording is the seam." Findings:

1. **What defers (the engine ‚Äî Clause 2).** The quarterly settlement *runs* (per-Producer/Supplier, cadence read from K); the **`ProducerSettlementStatementIssued` 5-section composition** (DEC-156 ‚Äî Section A per-Club / B Discovery / C refunds+clawbacks / D OC shares / E Direct-Purchase informational); the **OC 5% aggregation into Section D** (DEC-161/162); the **producer-fault clawback netting** (DEC-164/025, Section C); the **settlement-counterparty disambiguation routing** (DEC-161 ‚Äî Supplier vs OC Producer, two-statement case); the **producer settlement-currency option** (¬ß4.6); the **settlement-statement FSM** (`accruing‚Üícomposing‚Üíissued‚Üísettled`); and the **Xero AP routing** of the statement. These are deferred to **operator-run for the first cycle(s).**

2. **Why it is safely deferrable ‚Äî the structural reason.** The **first quarterly close lands months post-launch** (the settlement cadence is quarterly per DEC-042; the first full quarter of sell-through must accumulate before any statement is due). So at launch there is *no* settlement run to automate for months ‚Äî the whole engine can be operator-run first and automated after the first close. This is the same "first cycle is months out" logic that makes D4 safe.

3. **The recording is the seam (verified).** Module E keeps **recording** every settlement-input event the deferred engine will aggregate: it consumes Module D's ratified-KEPT `SupplierPaymentCompleted` (atomic per PO, D.24/Q5) + `InboundEventCostFinalized`; Module S's `DiscoveryRevenueShareAccrued` (the OC accrual, S.19) + `RefundIssued` (cause-tagged, S.26b) + `DiscoveryRevenueShareReversed` (S.26b); and routes each to Xero in real time (E.27). **The recording is NOT dropped** ‚Äî Module D ratified KEEP its recording (D.24), Module S ratified KEEP its emission (S.19), and Module E keeps consuming + recording. At launch the operator composes the first statement(s) manually from the recorded events + runs Xero AP manually; the engine reads the same recorded events when it is built ‚Äî purely additive.

4. **Honest weight + the floor it must not breach.** D19 is *genuinely* the heaviest in-module defer of the exercise after Module B's D12 ‚Äî the 5-section settlement engine + OC computation + clawback netting is "intricate Module E mechanics feeding Xero with strict immutability/credit-note discipline" (the grounding's heaviest-looking #6). The honest risk the kickoff flagged: **the defer must not drop the *recording*** ‚Äî it does not (point 3). And the **immutability/credit-note discipline** (E.27) the engine would feed is **floor ‚Äî KEPT** whether the engine is automated or operator-run (the operator's manual statements still route through Xero under post-sync immutability). **Partial PO settlement is already deferred (OQ-20)** ‚Äî `SupplierPaymentCompleted` atomic per PO; carry verbatim.

**Verdict:** DEFER the quarterly settlement *engine* ‚Üí operator-run first cycle(s); Module E keeps **recording** all settlement-input events (the seam, verified whole); the first quarterly close is months post-launch. Confirm + name the operator-run-settlement ops posture (Q1).

### ¬ß3.3 D21 ‚Äî chargeback ingestion KEPT (Paolo override of the locked dial ‚Äî payment automation from day 1)

The locked dial recommended **SIMPLIFY ‚Üí manual at launch.** **At ratification Paolo overrode it ‚Üí KEEP.** Findings + the override:

1. **What the dial proposed deferring.** v1.1 = the Airwallex chargeback webhook (DEC-168 step 1 `dispute.created` ‚Üí auto-record ‚Üí auto-emit `CustomerChargebackFlagged` ‚Üí K `CHARGEBACK_REVIEW` Hold; step 5 `dispute.resolved` ‚Üí auto-record `ChargebackResolved`/`ChargebackRecovered`). 2. **Why Paolo kept it.** The Airwallex integration is **floor from day 1** (Clause 1 ‚Äî customer charges/refunds cannot run without it). Paolo's directive: *"integration with airwallex from day 1 ‚Äî how can we process customer payment without integration?"* and the general principle **"payment automation should be KEPT."** This is the floor-protective calibration the kickoff asked for, exercised by Paolo: where automation rides existing day-1 infrastructure cheaply and the manual alternative opens a real gap, **KEEP it.**

3. **The outcome.** The **full 5-step chargeback chain auto-ingestion is KEPT from day 1** (E.24): auto-record on `dispute.created`, auto-emit `CustomerChargebackFlagged` ‚Üí K `CHARGEBACK_REVIEW` Hold, the 7-BD dispute SLA (DEC-047), auto-record on `dispute.resolved`, and the no-auto-lift discipline ‚Äî all automated. **Module K's side is unchanged (K.28 ‚Äî trigger-agnostic by design):** K consumes the `CustomerChargebackFlagged` event whether the trigger is automated (now, D21 KEPT) or manual (the dial's proposal). The boundary that survived: customer payment-processing automation (charge/refund/chargeback) = KEPT; the still-deferred items (D19 supplier-settlement engine, D4 INV3 dunning escalation) are *not* customer payment processing.

**Verdict (ratified):** **KEEP** the chargeback webhook auto-ingestion from day 1 ‚Äî payment automation is floor, not a Lean cut. The principle is recorded as the durable steer **"payment automation should be KEPT"** for Phase C + future finance scope (Q3 ‚Äî ratified KEEP).

### ¬ß3.4 D18 ‚Äî multi-currency dual-record (FLOOR ‚Äî not a candidate)

The locked dial: **KEEP (if D1 multi-currency).** Findings:

1. **The data-integrity heart of FX-correct refunds ‚Äî KEPT WHOLE.** Every customer-facing financial event recorded in customer currency **and** EUR; per-leg FX rate-lock at each leg's emission moment (DEC-169 E6-48); EOD Rome snapshot + admin buffer + daily refresh + Airwallex mid-market; **refund at the original captured rate (no fresh snapshot)**; `FXVarianceRecorded` for the Airwallex-vs-snapshot capture gap (E.26).

2. **D1 keeps all 5 currencies ‚Üí the machinery is fixed-cost.** D1 ratified KEEP all 5 (EUR/USD/GBP/CHF/JPY), so the dual-record machinery is kept **whole** ‚Äî and here is the honest finding the kickoff asked for: **narrowing the currency count would save ~nothing, because the dual-record machinery is fixed-cost regardless of count** (the per-leg rate-lock, the snapshot pipeline, the refund-at-original-rate logic, the variance recording are all the same whether 2 currencies or 5). So even if D1 had narrowed, the D18 saving would be marginal.

3. **Unsafe to simplify ‚Äî say so.** Simplifying the dual-record (e.g., single-currency recording, or refund-at-current-rate) would directly break FX-correct refunds ‚Äî a data-integrity failure that silently mis-refunds customers. **This is floor, not a candidate.** The kickoff is explicit: be honest where a simplification risks the floor; D18 is the clearest case.

**Verdict:** KEEP the multi-currency dual-record whole ‚Äî FLOOR; not a candidate. D1's 5 currencies make the machinery fixed-cost; narrowing saves nothing. Confirm (Q4).
